Local Food

  • Pecel Pitik: A sacred dish of the Osing tribe, made from spicy chicken with a special traditional sambal.
  • Sego Tempong: Rice served with various side dishes, boiled vegetables, fresh salad, and spicy sambal made from fresh ingredients like chili, tomato, and shrimp paste.
  • Sego Cawuk: Rice mixed with rich spiced fish broth and various side dishes such as steamed fish, egg, and grated coconut.
  • Rujak Soto: A unique combination of vegetable salad with yellow soto broth and petis (fermented shrimp paste).
  • Botok Tawon: An extreme dish made from honeycomb, perfect for culinary adventurers.
  • Sego Kalak: Mixed rice with spicy shredded chicken, egg, serundeng (fried coconut), saut√©ed vegetables, and spicy kalak sambal.
  • Ayam Kesrut: Chicken or beef in a clear spicy broth without coconut milk, named after the habit of holding back a runny nose while eating it.

Banyuwangi's traditional foods offer unique, spicy, and rich flavors, making them a special attraction for both tourists and locals.
